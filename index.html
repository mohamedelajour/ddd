Information about some tourist places


The Abu Simbel Temples :
- Two massive rock-cut temples in southern Egypt, built by Pharaoh Ramses II around 1264 BCE. - The main temple is dedicated to the gods Ra-Horakhty, Ptah, and Amun, with four colossal statues of Ramses II on the facade. - The smaller temple is dedicated to the goddess Hathor and Queen Nefertari, featuring six statues - two of Ramses II and four of Nefertari. - Originally located on the west bank of the Nile, the temples were relocated in the 1960s to save them from being submerged by the Aswan High Dam. - This was an immense engineering feat, carried out with the support of UNESCO. - Today, the Abu Simbel temples are a major tourist attraction, showcasing the grandeur of ancient Egyptian architecture and engineering.

Luxor is :
a city in southern Egypt that serves as the site of the ancient city of Thebes. It is located on the east bank of the Nile River and is home to numerous ancient Egyptian temples and tombs. Some key facts about Luxor: - Luxor is often referred to as the "world's greatest open-air museum" due to the sheer number and scale of its ancient monuments. - The city is home to the Karnak Temple Complex, one of the largest religious complexes in the world. It features massive columns, obelisks, and remnants of once-grand temples. - The Valley of the Kings, located on the west bank of the Nile, contains the tombs of numerous pharaohs from the New Kingdom period, including the famous tomb of Tutankhamun. - Other notable sites in Luxor include the Luxor Temple, the Colossi of Memnon, and the Temple of Hatshepsut. - Luxor experiences a hot desert climate, with very hot summers and mild winters. The best time to visit is typically in the cooler months between October and April. - Tourism is a major industry in Luxor, which draws visitors from around the world to see its unparalleled archaeological treasures from ancient Egyptian civilization. The Valley of the Kings :

is a ancient Egyptian archaeological site located on the west bank of the Nile River, opposite the city of Luxor. It was used as a burial site for pharaohs and powerful nobles of the New Kingdom period (c.1550-1069 BC). The valley contains over 60 tombs, the most famous of which is the tomb of Tutankhamun, which was discovered largely intact in 1922. Other notable tombs include those of Ramesses II, Seti I, and Amenhotep III. The tombs were cut into the valley's limestone hillsides and contain elaborate wall paintings and relief carvings depicting the pharaohs' lives and their journey to the afterlife. They were equipped with all the material goods the pharaohs would need in the next world. The Valley of the Kings is considered one of the most important archaeological sites in the world, as it has provided an unparalleled insight into the burial practices and beliefs of ancient Egyptian royalty. It remains a major tourist attraction in Egypt today.

The Pyramids :

of Giza are one of the most iconic and famous archaeological sites in the world. Located on the outskirts of Cairo, Egypt, the Giza pyramid complex consists of the three major pyramids - the Great Pyramid of Giza, the Pyramid of Khafre, and the Pyramid of Menkaure. The Great Pyramid of Giza, also known as the Pyramid of Khufu or Cheops, is the oldest and largest of the three main pyramids. It is the oldest and largest of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World and the only one that remains largely intact. The pyramids were constructed as monumental tombs for the pharaohs of Egypt's Old Kingdom over 4,500 years ago. They were built using millions of precisely cut and fitted limestone and granite blocks, a feat of engineering that continues to amaze modern observers. Alongside the pyramids are the iconic Sphinx, a massive stone statue with the head of a human and the body of a lion, as well as several smaller satellite pyramids, temples, and cemeteries. The Giza pyramid complex is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Egypt, drawing millions of visitors each year to marvel at these ancient wonders. Even after thousands of years, the pyramids remain a powerful symbol of the ingenuity and ambition of the ancient Egyptian civilization.

KING TUTANKHAMUN

date of birth : 1341.Bc

- Tutankhamun was an ancient Egyptian pharaoh who reigned during the Eighteenth Dynasty, and he ascended to the throne at a young age, around 18 years old. - His famous tomb was discovered in the Valley of the Kings in Luxor in 1922 by the British archaeologist Howard Carter, and his tomb was one of the most important archaeological discoveries of the 20th century - Tutankhamun's tomb was well-preserved and revealed many precious artifacts and magnificent arts from his short reign, which provided a clear picture of the life and culture of ancient Egypt. - Tutankhamun is considered one of the most famous pharaohs of ancient Egypt, and he played an important role in Egyptian history despite his short reign, which did not exceed 10 years.




QUEEN CLEOPATRA

date of birth : 69.Bc

- Cleopatra VII Philopator was the last active Egyptian pharaoh, ruling from 51 BC to 30 BC. - She was part of the Ptolemaic dynasty and was renowned for her intelligence, ambition, and romantic liaisons with the Roman leaders Julius Caesar and Mark Antony. - Cleopatra played a pivotal role in the power struggles between Rome and Egypt during the end of the Roman Republic. Her alliances with Caesar and Antony were attempts to preserve Egypt's independence. - Cleopatra's rule and romantic relationships with the Roman leaders were heavily romanticized in popular culture. She is one of the most famous figures from ancient Egypt. - In 30 BC, after the defeat of Mark Antony's forces by Octavian (the future Roman emperor Augustus), Cleopatra committed suicide, reportedly by allowing an asp (venomous snake) to bite her.



ROSETTA STONE

196.Bc

The Rosetta Stone is an ancient Egyptian granodiorite stone inscribed with a decree issued at Memphis in 196 BC on behalf of King Ptolemy V. The decree is written in three scripts - ancient Egyptian hieroglyphic, Demotic, and ancient Greek. The stone was discovered in 1799 by French soldiers near the town of Rosetta (Rashid) in the Nile Delta region of Egypt. The parallel text in the three different scripts provided the key to deciphering Egyptian hieroglyphics, which had been a mystery for centuries. The Rosetta Stone is considered one of the most important archaeological discoveries, as it allowed scholars to begin translating and understanding ancient Egyptian writing for the first time. It is currently housed in the British Museum in London and is one of the most popular exhibits there.
